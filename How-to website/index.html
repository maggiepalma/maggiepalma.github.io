How-to prepare for a journalism internship interview

Applying for internships can be overwhelming and frustrating. When you are offered an interview it may be nervewracking and scary. There are so many sites with broad advice to prepare to any interview, but this site will help you prepare for an interview specific to journalism internships.

photo by Alex Green via pexel

Before interview

Research the company

A great place to start preparing for your interview is to take time to learn about the company’s mission, vision and coals. The knowledge you get from this research is a fantastic way to show you’re interested in the position, provide context for your conversation and create thoughtful answers for each question.

Prepare a story/pitch about yourself

One of the first things an interviewer will ask you to do is to introduce yourself. Be ready for it and provide a quick overview of yourself and your skills. When creating your short answer make sure to use impactful key words/events to describe who you are and your interests. Make sure to practice this in front a friend or family member until your answer comes naturally and confident.

Examine the job description

Use the posted job description as a guide to your list of qualifications, qualities and background. These characteristics are what the employer is looking for in an ideal candidate, so the more boxes you check off the more the employee will be able to see that you are qualified. Be ready to explain your qualifications to your interviewer.

Create a portfolio/samples of your work

As a journalist it is always a great idea to have clips ready of your best work. They can show the employer the stories you are most proud of and how great of a journalist you are. Bring them with you as well as your resume and cover letter to give to the interviewer.

How to create a portfolio

Write questions for the end of the interview

Creating a thoughtful list of questions about the company and job is important to help you decide if the internship meets your needs while also showing you are genuinely interested in the position. Some examples are:

  • What is the work environment like?
  • What is the biggest challenge of the position?
  • What is the difference between an intern and a full-time employee?
  • How would you describe a typical day in this position?
Some common questions

Create a story pitch

It is a good idea to come up with a couple of stories that could fit the group you are interviewing with. To do so it is good to keep up with news, trends and events going on around the world/locally. This shows you took the time to read some of the stories the company published and some strong pieces that would benefit their publication.

More tips

Dressing the part

Whether the interview is online or in person it is extremely important to dress appropriately for it. Take time before your interview to put together an outfit that is professional.
